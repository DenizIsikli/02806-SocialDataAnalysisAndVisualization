Week 7 - Course Summary

Week 1 - Introduction & Data Overview

Introduced the San Francisco crime dataset and explored its structure, scope, and limitations.

  • Examined time span, categories, and dataset structure
  • Identified missing values and inconsistencies
  • Created initial plots to understand overall trends

Week 2 - Temporal Patterns

Analyzed how crime varies over time, revealing strong daily and weekly patterns.

  • Explored hourly and weekly variation
  • Compared patterns across crime types
  • Observed clear differences between daytime and nighttime activity

Week 3 - Distributions & Conditional Patterns

Investigated statistical distributions and how crime patterns differ across districts.

  • Used jitter plots, box plots, and QQ plots
  • Compared crime across police districts
  • Identified distinct distribution patterns

Week 4 - Spatial Analysis & Correlation

Explored spatial patterns and relationships between different crime types.

  • Mapped crime distribution across the city
  • Identified concentrated hotspots
  • Analyzed correlations between crime categories

Week 5 - Geographical Visualization

Developed interactive maps to better understand spatial crime patterns.

  • Built maps using Plotly and Folium
  • Visualized geographic concentration of crime
  • Compared different mapping techniques

Week 6 - Interactive Visualization

Focused on interactive and animated visualizations to explore temporal dynamics.

  • Created interactive Plotly charts
  • Built animated visualizations over time
  • Observed stable daily crime rhythms

Week 7 - Website

Designed and implemented a website to communicate analysis results clearly.

  • Set up GitHub Pages deployment
  • Structured content using HTML and CSS
  • Focused on clarity and presentation

Week 8 - Data Story

Combined analysis and visualization into a short narrative data story.

  • Selected a focused insight from previous work
  • Built three complementary visualizations
  • Presented results in a linear, author-driven format
