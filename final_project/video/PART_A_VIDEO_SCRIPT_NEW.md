# NYC 311 Service Requests: 1-Minute Concept Video (Intro-First)

---

## NARRATION SCRIPT FOR TEXT-TO-SPEECH

*new slide* Central Idea: "What Is Everyday Urban Friction?"

This project asks: what daily problems make New Yorkers ask the city for help, and what do those requests reveal?

*new slide* Why This Is Interesting

NYC 311 data (the city's non-emergency request system) captures daily life at scale and links residents, infrastructure, and city response.

*new slide* Dataset Snapshot: Size and Properties

The dataset has 50,000 requests from 2020 to 2025, across 5 boroughs and 514 community boards, with 10 variables and no missing values in this sample.

*new slide* Preliminary Visualization Mockup

The final product is an interactive dashboard: a map of where complaints happen, a chart of top types, and a timeline of demand.

*new slide* Quick Data Preview: Fundamental Distributions

Quick preview: HEAT, WATER, and RODENT make up about one-third of complaints. Borough shares differ, and winter looks higher.

*new slide* Genre and Why It Fits

Genre: data-driven exploratory narrative, following Segel and Heer. It fits because we start with context, show early patterns, and end with open questions.

*new slide* What Comes Next

In the full project, we will test how patterns vary by place and time, and design visuals that are fair and easy to understand.
